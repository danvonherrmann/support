Cable Boxes / Receivers

DISH Wally Receiver

Description: Small black rectangular box with "Wally" branding. Most common receiver in SecureVision properties.

Connections & Inputs:

  • Power Input: AC power cord (back of box)
  • Satellite In: Coaxial cable connection (screw-type)
  • HDMI Out: Connects to TV (best quality)
  • USB Ports: For external storage/playback
  • Ethernet: Network connection (optional)

Front Panel Buttons:

  • Power: Turn receiver on/off
  • System Info: Display receiver & card IDs
  • Locate Remote: Makes remote beep

DISH ViP 211 Receiver

Description: Older silver/black rectangular box with "DISH" branding and blue display screen.

Connections & Inputs:

  • Power Input: AC power cord (back of box)
  • Satellite In: Coaxial cable connection
  • HDMI Out: Digital video/audio to TV
  • Component Out: Red/Green/Blue video cables
  • Composite Out: Yellow/Red/White cables
  • Optical Audio: Digital audio output

Front Panel Buttons:

  • Power: Turn receiver on/off
  • Info: Display program information
  • Select: Make selections
  • Up/Down: Channel navigation

OnStream M2 Evolve

Description: Modern black streaming receiver with sleek design. Used in select properties with OnStream service.

Connections & Inputs:

  • Power Input: AC adapter connection
  • HDMI Out: Main connection to TV
  • Ethernet: Wired network connection
  • WiFi: Built-in wireless capability
  • USB: For updates and media

Remote Controls

DISH 52.0/54.0 Remote (Wally Remote)

Description: Black remote with red trim along the side. Voice control capable. Most common in newer installations.

Key Buttons & Functions:

  • Home: Main menu & guide access
  • Voice: Voice search & commands
  • TV (side): Switch to TV control mode
  • Input (side): Change TV inputs/sources
  • SAT (side): Switch to satellite control mode
  • Volume +/-: Adjust TV volume (when paired)
  • Power: Turn TV on/off (when paired)
  • Info: Display program information
  • Guide: Channel guide & schedule
  • Options: Additional settings menu
  • DVR: Recorded programs (if equipped)
  • Skip Back/Forward: 10-second skip buttons
  • Rewind/Fast Forward: Playback control
  • Play/Pause: Control playback
  • Record: Record programs (if equipped)
  • Number Pad: Direct channel entry
  • Dash (-): Enter sub-channels (e.g., 5-1)
üí° Pro Tip: The buttons on the SIDE of the remote (TV, Input, SAT) are the most important for troubleshooting!

DISH 40.0 Remote (Older Models)

Description: Black/silver remote with white TV button at top. Used with ViP receivers and older installations.

Key Buttons & Functions:

  • TV (top): Switch to TV control mode (white button)
  • Input (top): Change TV inputs/sources
  • SAT (top): Switch to satellite mode
  • Mode Buttons: Switch between TV/VCR/AUX modes
  • Power: Turn devices on/off
  • Volume +/-: Adjust volume (when programmed)
  • Mute: Silence audio
  • Info: Display program information
  • Menu: Access main menu
  • Guide: Channel guide
  • Arrow Keys: Navigate menus
  • Select: Confirm selections
  • Cancel: Go back/exit
  • DVR Controls: Pause, rewind, fast forward
  • Number Pad: Direct channel entry
  • Recall: Return to previous channel
  • # (Pound): Confirms programming codes

TV on Wrong Input / No Signal ‚ñº

Symptoms: Black screen, blue screen, "No Signal" message, stuck on smart TV apps (Roku, Netflix, etc.)

üí° This is the #1 most common issue! Your TV is simply on the wrong input. This is quick and easy to fix.

Method 1: Using Your DISH Remote

  1. Find your DISH remote. It should be black with red trim (newer Wally remote) or black/silver (older remote).
  2. Press the TV button once. On Wally remotes, this is on the side. On older remotes, it's a white button at the top.
  3. Press the Input button repeatedly. Also on the side of Wally remotes, or below the TV button on older remotes.
  4. Wait 2-3 seconds on each input that appears (HDMI 1, HDMI 2, etc.) until you see your DISH picture.
  5. Press the SAT button when the picture appears. This is critical! The screen may say "Press SAT to watch TV."

Method 2: Using Your TV's Remote

  1. Find your TV's original remote (Samsung, LG, Vizio, etc.)
  2. Press the Source or Input button on the TV remote.
  3. Use arrow keys to select each HDMI input one by one until the DISH picture appears.
  4. Press SAT on your DISH remote once the picture is restored.

Method 3: Using TV Buttons

  1. Look for physical buttons on your TV. They're usually on the side, back, or bottom edge.
  2. Find and press the Input or Source button repeatedly until DISH appears.
  3. Press SAT on your DISH remote to complete the process.
‚ö†Ô∏è Important Final Step Always press the SAT button after getting your picture back, or the remote won't change channels!

WiFi Connection Issues ‚ñº

Symptoms: No internet, slow connection, can't find WiFi network, asking for password

Finding Your WiFi Network

Your WiFi network name (SSID) typically includes your property name or unit number. Look for networks like "SecureVision-[Property]" or similar.

üí° WiFi Password Information Passwords are case-sensitive! Make sure to enter capital and lowercase letters exactly as shown. If you need your WiFi password, please call us at 251-967-4455 with your property name and unit number.

Connection Problems

  1. Forget and reconnect to the network. Go to your device's WiFi settings, select your network, choose "Forget," then reconnect with your password.
  2. Restart your device. Turn your phone, tablet, or laptop completely off and back on.
  3. Check for service alerts. There may be a building-wide internet outage. Contact us to check network status.
  4. Try multiple devices. If only one device has issues, the problem is likely with that device, not the network.

Slow Speeds or Buffering

  1. Move closer to the router if possible. WiFi strength decreases with distance and obstacles.
  2. Check how many devices are connected. Many devices using WiFi simultaneously can slow speeds.
  3. Restart your device to establish a fresh connection.
  4. Contact us if issues persist. We can remotely reboot your router or send a technician if needed.
üì± Pro Tip: Toggle Airplane Mode On phones and tablets, turning Airplane Mode on for 10 seconds, then off again, can help re-establish a better connection.

"Complete Signal Loss" or "Acquiring Signal" ‚ñº

Symptoms: TV shows "Complete Signal Loss," "Partial Signal Loss," or is stuck on "Acquiring Signal" screen

‚ö†Ô∏è First Check Make sure your TV is on the correct input first! See the "TV on Wrong Input" section above. If you see a DISH error message (not just "No Signal"), continue below.

Running a System Scan

  1. Check cable connections. Make sure the coaxial cable (screws in) is tight at both the wall and back of the DISH box.
  2. Access diagnostics:
    • Wally Remote: Press the Home button 3 times, go to "Dish" > "Test Installation"
    • Older Remote: Press Menu, then dial 6-1-1 on the keypad
  3. Start the scan/test. This can take 5-15 minutes. Don't interrupt it!
  4. After the scan: If channels still aren't working, unplug the DISH box from power for 30 seconds, then plug it back in.
üí° What if the scan fails? If you see "fewer satellites detected," select "Cancel" (NOT Save), then do a power cycle. If problems continue, please call us.

Remote Control Not Working ‚ñº

Symptoms: Remote won't change channels, won't control TV volume/power, unresponsive buttons

Basic Fixes (Try These First!)

  1. Replace batteries. This fixes most remote issues! If no lights appear when you press buttons, batteries are definitely dead.
  2. Press the SAT button. If the remote won't change channels but works otherwise, you're likely in TV mode. Press the SAT button once to return to satellite control.
  3. Check line of sight. Point the remote directly at the DISH receiver (not the TV) when changing channels.

Re-Pairing Remote to DISH Box

  1. Locate the DISH receiver (the box under or near the TV).
  2. Press the "Locate Remote" button on the front of the box. Your remote should start beeping.
  3. If it doesn't beep: Press the "System Info" button on the box, then press and hold the SAT button on your remote until it beeps.

Programming Remote for TV Volume/Power

Wally Remote (Black with red trim):

  1. Press the Home button twice
  2. Go to Settings > Remote Control > TV
  3. Select "TV Pairing Wizard"
  4. Choose your TV brand and test the codes provided

Older Remote (40.0):

  1. Press and hold the TV button until all other buttons flash
  2. Enter your TV brand's 3-digit code (examples below)
  3. Press the # (pound) key
  4. Test volume up/down buttons
üí° Common TV Codes
  • Samsung: 504, 506, 654, 794
  • Vizio: 505, 627
  • LG: 512, 766
  • Sony: 502, 639
  • If one code doesn't work, try the others for your brand!

Missing Channels / "Programming Not Authorized" ‚ñº

Symptoms: Only see a few channels, pay-per-view, DishScapes, or "Programming Not Authorized" message

üì° What's happening? Your receiver needs to receive an "activation signal" from the provider. This happens when the box has been unplugged for a while or after certain resets.

Getting Your Channels Back

  1. Don't worry - this is normal! The receiver just needs a refresh signal from us.
  2. Call SecureVision at 251-967-4455. We'll need to send an activation "hit" to your receiver.
  3. We'll need two ID numbers from your receiver:
    • Press the System Info button on the front of the DISH box, or
    • Press Home 3 times (Wally) or Menu twice (older remote)
    • Read us the Receiver ID (starts with 'R') and Smart Card ID (starts with 'S')
  4. Leave the receiver powered on. It takes 15-45 minutes for channels to be restored after we send the signal.
  5. Try a reboot if needed. If channels haven't returned after 30 minutes, unplug the box for 30 seconds and plug it back in.

General Internet Connection Steps ‚ñº

Connecting to WiFi

  1. Open WiFi settings on your device. On most devices, this is in Settings > WiFi (or Network).
  2. Make sure WiFi is turned ON. Some devices have a physical switch or button to enable wireless.
  3. Look for your property's network. Network names typically include your property name or unit number.
  4. Select your network and enter the password (case-sensitive!).
  5. Wait for connection. It may take 10-30 seconds to connect.
  6. Open a web browser and try loading a website to confirm you're online.
üí° Can't see your network?
  • Move closer to where you think the router might be (often in closets or near the TV)
  • Make sure your device's WiFi is enabled
  • Try turning WiFi off and on again on your device
  • The network may be temporarily offline - contact us to check

Understanding Network Names

SecureVision WiFi networks may appear as:

  • SecureVision-[PropertyName]
  • [PropertyName]-WiFi
  • SVI-[BuildingName]
  • Or a variation including your building or unit identifier
